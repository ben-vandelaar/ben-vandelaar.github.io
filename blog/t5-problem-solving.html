Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:

  1. Got blocked on a simple problem
  2. Solved a problem in an elegant way In both cases:

What was the problem?
It was during the 4.0 Free codecamp challange.. a few of those to be honest. But one of the ones that tripped me up was Basic JavaScript: Record Collection.
What problem solving techniques did you use?
I tried everything... What I found helped most though was finding videos that described the subject
matter in different ways and walk you through similar questions. I find that sometimes the hardest
part of the question is actually figuring out what they want or what they're actually asking for.

How did you feel throughout the process?.
It could be a little stressfull felt like a comvaluted speed bump with this question. You sale down understanding everything answering the questions and then out of no where it's like "wait. what?" I found it important to write everthing down. Keep calm. Breath. And slowly figure it out.


What did you learn?
That I've still got a lot to learn and I'm getting there but it's a slightly different way of thinking. I've been making sure every now and again to sit down and read a book on coding, or watch a video or just something away from the sprints to try and get more programmer minded.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:
Pseudocode:It's always good to re write out the question in a form of code and to break
it down I a way that's easier to understand.
Trying something: Coding is essential trial and error you should always try reload the
page and see what happend. If it worked yay if not make notes on why not and try again.
Rubber ducky method: Haven't tried this one too much. But maybe I should..
Reading error messages: This is a really good way to see what's happening under the
hood and then being able to make live changes to things to see how far out you were and know
exactly what lines of code are stopping your program from running.
Console.logging: This is a good way to see of your functions are running the way they
should. Passing a variable that you know the answer to and making sure that's what you get out.
You can then test other areas of the functions and make sure what's being logged it correct.
Googling: Sadly still my go to. Can save time and someone has normally asked the same
question you have though sometimes the answers aren't the best. But it's normally a sure fire to see where you went wrong.
Asking your peers for help:At my old job when I was working in Python scripting small
tools for Maya there was always someone with a wealth of knowledge and understanding far beyond
mine around to help so asking a "Peer" was always the go to option for me. It help more I think
than the likes of google as you can pick the person's brain and ask more in depth questions and
actually get taught how to do it as opposed to just showing the answer.
Improving your process with reflection: Reflection is key it gives you a chance to see
where you struggled and how you struggled, then hopefully if you're still game find ways so you
don't struggle on the same kind of questions or areas again. This can be tricky as it's normally a
little deeper than just ah 2+2=4 it's more why does 2+2=4 and what about my way of thinking didn't
allow me to see that answer... If that makes sense..
