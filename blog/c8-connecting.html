Complete the political awareness exercise here in writing.

1. Think of a difficult situation from your present or past, when there was some conflict or disagreement, something real, something that has some meaning and potency for you.

This is hard.. I think the last time I actually argued for something and not just to show another point of view, was not too long ago I was working as a producer on a short film. The Director had got the 1st AD to come and tell me we will be dropping some of the breaks in order to make up to lost time...

2. Describe the situation as though you are 100 percent correct and reasonable. Do that either in writing or by talking about it in a monologue.

There's no way I was going to allow that to happen even if certain members of the crew were ok with it. I told the director
no. Why? Because 1. I have worked in positions like these and you normally just agree to work no break because you'd
lose your job otherwise. 2. It's law. If people are working 8hr days they're initialed to 1hr rest. At least 15mins for
morning tea 30mins for lunch and 15 for afternoon tea. This means also if anyone on our crew went home tired overworked
and crashed, who gets the blame we do. And I'm pretty sure it's jail time. There were a few more reasons I
argued it but I came down to the health and safety of the crew and saving mine and the directors asses.


3. Now describe the situation as though the other person is (or the other people are) 100 percent correct and reasonable. Do
that either in writing or by talking about it in a monologue.

We needed to make up more time. Each shot each set up is taking waaay longer than we had anticipated. There's no quick
insert shots, that's a 15min set up. I've already cut too many sequences out of the film and we really can't afford to
make any more. We have to be done by Saturday we already need to change the lead actors flight times. The DOP said he's
happy to work though to lunch since we're on a roll. And so does the 1st AD.

I know what the director was feeling. There was also the argument that having a break stops the flow and that takes a while
to get started back up once it's gone. Which is true. A team of 16+ people, it takes a while to find the rhythm
and to keep stopping and starting only hampers the progress. We came to a compromise I moved the 15min morning tea and
combined it with lunch making it 45mins. I made sure to bring out food and drinks to people on set and make sure
the communication between the crew is kept up so that if someone needed a break they could.
